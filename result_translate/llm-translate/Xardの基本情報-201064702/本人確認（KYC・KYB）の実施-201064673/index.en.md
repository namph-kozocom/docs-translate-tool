In Xard, there are cases where the partner conducts KYC as part of the identity verification process and cases where KYC is not conducted under certain conditions.

KYC stands for "Know Your Customer" and refers to identity verification to prevent crimes such as money laundering. In this manual, it refers to verifying identity items in compliance with the Act on Prevention of Transfer of Criminal Proceeds.

The confirmation items in KYC and the conditions when KYC is not conducted (nonKYC) are as follows:

## 1\. Confirmation items in KYC

KYC is generally conducted by the partner. Under the Act on Prevention of Transfer of Criminal Proceeds, KYC requires confirmation of the following identity items for the representative of the corporation and the corporation itself.

**Representative of the corporation**

* Name
    
* Residence
    
* Date of birth
    

**Corporation**

* Name
    
* Location of head office or principal office
    

* Check the detailed requirements for conducting KYC with your partner.

## 2\. Conditions when KYC is not conducted (nonKYC)

Issuers of prepaid payment instruments are not considered specific businesses under the Act on Prevention of Transfer of Criminal Proceeds, thus legally do not have the obligation to KYC. However, if KYC is not conducted (nonKYC), international brands require the following usage restrictions.

The usage restrictions for nonKYC by Visa and JCB are as follows.

**Visa**

* Transaction limit per transaction: **30,000 yen**
    
* Transaction limit per month: **120,000 yen**
    
* Transaction limit within validity period: **1,000,000 yen**
    
* Refusal of overseas in-store transactions
    

**JCB**

* Prepaid card usage limit: **100,000 yen**
    
* Usage limit per month: **100,000 yen**
    
* Usage limit per transaction: **1,000,000 yen**
