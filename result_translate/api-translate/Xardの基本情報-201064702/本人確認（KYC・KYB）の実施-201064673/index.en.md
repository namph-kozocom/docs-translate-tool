At Xard, partners may perform KYC as part of the identity verification process, or may not perform KYC if certain conditions are met.

KYC is an abbreviation for "Know Your Customer" and refers to identity verification to prevent crimes such as money laundering.This manual refers to the confirmation of personal identification information in accordance with the Act on Prevention of Transfer of Criminal Proceeds (Criminal Proceeds Act).

The items to be confirmed during KYC and the conditions when KYC is not implemented (nonKYC) are as follows:

## 1\. KYC confirmation items

KYC is basically carried out by the partner.KYC under the Criminal Proceeds Act requires corporate representatives and corporations to confirm the following personal identification information.

**Representative of the corporation**

* Name

* Residence

* Date of birth


**Corporate**

* Name

* Location of head office or principal office


*Please check with your partner for detailed KYC implementation requirements.

*Please refer to the following page for KYC implementation requirements.
"Documents required to confirm personal identification matters and confirmation methods" in JAFIC's "[Outline of the Act on Prevention of Transfer of Criminal Proceeds] (https://www.npa.go.jp/sosikihanzai/jafic/hourei/law_com.htm)"

## 2\. Conditions when KYC is not implemented (nonKYC)

Businesses that issue prepaid payment instruments do not fall under the category of specified business operators under the Criminal Proceeds Act, so they are not legally required to perform KYC.However, if KYC is not implemented (nonKYC), international brands will request the following usage restrictions.

The restrictions on the use of nonKYC for Visa and JCB are as follows.

**Visa**

* Payment limit per transaction: **30,000 yen**

* Payment limit per month: **120,000 yen**

* Payment limit within validity period: **1 million yen**

* Reject overseas physical store payments


**JCB**

* Prepaid card usage limit: **100,000 yen**

* Maximum usage amount per month: **100,000 yen**

* Maximum usage amount per time: **1 million yen**
*One-time transaction amount at a funds transfer company under the Funds Settlement Act


* * *

As stated above, please set the usage limit within the range of the maximum amount, and set an appropriate amount from the perspective of consumer protection and prevention of unauthorized use.
